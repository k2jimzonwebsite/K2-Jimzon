# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary audience is Filipino consumers who buy Italian groceries, coffee, sweets, and pantry goods — people who have been buying through Shopee, Lazada, and Viber sellers and are tired of near-expiry stock, screenshot-based payments, and "is this legit?" doubt. They arrive on mobile as often as desktop, usually mid-consideration, comparing a known marketplace listing against buying direct.

Two secondary audiences share the same storefront but enter with different intent: wholesale buyers (cafés, restaurants, and resellers) who need tier pricing, live stock, and self-serve reordering; and pasabuy requesters who want a specific Italian product the store doesn't yet stock. The surface speaks first to the retail shopper — the other two are deliberate side doors, not the main room.

## Product Purpose

K2 Jimzon sells authentic Italian products that the company buys itself from Italian retail and wholesale suppliers and flies to Manila on its own monthly consignment. Every listing shows batch and best-before dates and live stock synced across channels. Success is a first-time visitor trusting the store enough to buy direct instead of defaulting back to a marketplace seller — and coming back next month knowing the stock count on screen is real.

## Positioning

We fly authentic Italian goods to Manila ourselves every month, and the stock you see is the stock we have — no near-expiry surprises, no group-chat ordering, no counterfeit pretense.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: Browse this month's arrivals — lead with the monthly-flight freshness angle ("what just landed from Milan"), then convert to purchase.
- Secondary CTA: Request a pasabuy — for visitors not ready to buy from stock, capture intent by letting them ask for anything from Italy.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: real Italian goods, bought in Italy and flown to Manila every month — with the stock count you can actually trust.
- Belief ladder: (1) these are genuinely authentic EU products, not counterfeit or grey-market; (2) they're fresh, because the store flies them in monthly and prints the dates; (3) the live stock and self-serve checkout are more reliable than the marketplace-and-Viber routine; (4) buying direct is the better deal and the safer one.
- Proof on hand: verified marketplace reviews and testimonials in `src/data/site.js` (retail, wholesale, and pasabuy voices); "4.9★ across 3,000+ marketplace ratings" and a five-year track record; per-listing batch and best-before dates; honest "inspired scent" labeling on the fragrance line as a trust signal in its own right.

## Brand Personality

Trusted, clean-luxury, and friendly. The voice is honest and a little dry — it names the annoyances of the old way plainly ("no screenshot-sending, no 'sent na po'") without turning shrill. Clean luxury shows in restraint: generous white space, editorial serif-and-sans typography, real photography, and a quiet Italian-red accent rather than decoration for its own sake. Warmth keeps it human and local, never cold or corporate. The feeling to evoke is confident reassurance: the calm of dealing with someone who clearly knows the goods and won't oversell them.

## Anti-references

- Generic Shopee/Lazada seller pages: badge spam, neon discount stickers, cluttered listing grids, and screenshot-based trust.
- Loud dropship / deal sites: countdown timers, "ONLY 2 LEFT!!" fake scarcity, gradient buttons, and manufactured urgency.
- Cold SaaS / dashboard templates: Inter-for-everything, purple gradients, card-in-card nesting, and the soulless-startup look.

Note: literal Italian-flag clichés (tricolore stripes everywhere, Tuscan-villa stock, pizza-parlor kitsch) are also off the table — Italian identity is carried by typography, photography, and a single quiet red accent, never by waving the flag.

## Design Principles

- **Show the receipts.** Every trust claim is backed by something concrete on screen — a date, a stock count, a real review, an honest label. Never ask the visitor to take authenticity on faith.
- **Quiet confidence over loud persuasion.** Restraint is the sell. No urgency theater, no badge spam; the calm, well-kept surface is itself the proof that this store is different from the marketplace.
- **Editorial, not catalog.** Treat the storefront like a well-designed magazine about the goods — hierarchy, photography, and typography do the work, not a wall of uniform product cards.
- **Honest by default.** When something is an "inspired scent" or a pasabuy quote, say so plainly. Transparency is the brand's competitive edge, so the design should never obscure a caveat.
- **One room, three doors.** Retail leads; wholesale and pasabuy are clearly signposted side paths that never crowd the main shopping experience or confuse the first-time visitor.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal conformance target; hold to strong best-effort practices. Keep body-text contrast comfortably readable (the muted-gray-on-tint trap is the one to avoid), maintain visible keyboard focus states, and honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for every animation — the existing CSS already does this and it should stay that way. Design for mobile-first reading, since much of the audience arrives on phones.
